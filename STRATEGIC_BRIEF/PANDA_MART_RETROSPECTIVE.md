## Five Failure Vectors — Mapped to Nine Components

### Failure Vector 1: Direct Import Liability — No Australian Compliance Buffer
**Root Cause:** Products sourced directly from overseas manufacturers without Australian-registered distributor. Retailer became de facto importer of record with 100% ACCC liability.
**Component Prevention:** Component 8 (Importer-of-Record Verification) — hard gate before any direct-import commercial relationship.
**Approved Website Language:** Component 8 copy from §3.3 (see NINE_COMPONENTS_SPECIFICATION.md)

### Failure Vector 2: 130 ESV Electrical Safety Charges — RCM Violations
**Root Cause:** Electrical goods sourced without verifying RCM registration on EESS database. Products without RCM cannot legally be sold in Australia.
**Component Prevention:** Component 3 (Category-Specific Mapping) classifies electrical goods as Tier 1. Mandatory EESS check before any PO. Component 1 (Product Verification) blocks non-RCM electrical goods at gate.
**Approved Website Language:** Component 3 copy from §3.3 (see NINE_COMPONENTS_SPECIFICATION.md)

### Failure Vector 3: Button Battery Accessibility Charges
**Root Cause:** Products containing accessible button batteries without child-resistant packaging, in violation of mandatory button battery safety standards.
**Component Prevention:** Component 1 + Component 7 require specific button battery accessibility testing (AS/NZS standards) before any product in scope is ranged.

### Failure Vector 4: Unlabelled Projectile Toys and Banned Products
**Root Cause:** Products not verified against AS/NZS toy safety standards. Banned products included in range without detection.
**Component Prevention:** Component 1 requires standard identification before ranging. Banned products identified in compliance database cannot pass verification. Component 7 requires third-party test reports for all children's product categories.

### Failure Vector 5: No Systemic Compliance Improvement Mechanism
**Root Cause:** Once violations identified, no root-cause analysis mechanism existed to prevent recurrence. Same compliance gaps persisted until enforcement action.
**Component Prevention:** Component 9 (Compliance Culture & Continuous Improvement) installs post-incident root-cause analysis as a standing process. First violation triggers system review. Second violation from same root cause represents systemic Component 9 failure.
**Approved Website Language:** Component 9 copy from §3.3 (see NINE_COMPONENTS_SPECIFICATION.md)

## The $50M Prevention Calculation (V7 §3.2)

The nine-component architecture is **documentable prevention**. At point of regulatory enforcement, the retailer with the full architecture can produce:

1. Complete compliance file for every ranged product (Component 5)
2. Supplier compliance audit record for every supplier (Component 2)
3. Third-party test reports for all Tier 1 products (Component 7)
4. Importer-of-record documentation with liability mapping (Component 8)
5. Documented compliance training records for all staff (Component 9)
6. Post-incident root-cause analysis records (Component 9)

> "The ACCC and courts treat the existence and quality of a compliance management system as a material factor in both the decision to prosecute and in penalty quantum." — V7 §3.2
